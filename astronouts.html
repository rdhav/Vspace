VSPACE ASTRONAUTS

Neil Armstrong

Neil Alden Armstrong (August 5, 1930 – August 25, 2012) was an American astronaut and aeronautical engineer who in 1969 became the first person to walk on the Moon. He was also a naval aviator, test pilot, and university professor.

Time in Space :

8 Days 14 Hours 12 Minutes

Neil Armstrong

  • Neil Armstrong was an American astronaut who became the first human to set foot on the moon on July 20, 1969, as part of the Apollo 11 mission.
  • Before becoming an astronaut, Armstrong was a highly experienced test pilot with the United States Air Force, involved in various aviation research projects.
  • In addition to being part of the Apollo 11 mission, Armstrong was also involved in the Gemini 8 mission in 1966, where he successfully completed his tasks despite experiencing life-threatening incidents.
  • After retiring from NASA, Neil Armstrong became a professor at the University of Cincinnati, where he taught aerospace engineering and was also involved in aviation research projects.
  • Armstrong was known as a humble figure and rarely gave interviews to the media after the Apollo 11 mission, preferring to maintain privacy and focus on his work as an educator and aviation enthusiast.

Buzz Aldrin

Buzz Aldrin is known as one of the American astronauts who landed on the moon as part of the Apollo 11 mission in 1969. Aldrin was born on January 20, 1930, in Glen Ridge, New Jersey, and indeed graduated from the United States Military Academy at West Point in 1951 with a degree in mechanical engineering.

Time in Space :

12 days 1 hour, and 53 minutes.

Buzz Aldrin

  • Name: Buzz Aldrin's original name is Edwin Eugene Aldrin Jr. He adopted the nickname "Buzz" when he was young, and officially changed his name to Buzz in 1988.
  • Taekwondo Practitioner on the Moon: Buzz Aldrin is the first person to perform taekwondo exercises in space. During the Apollo 11 mission, Aldrin brought a small black belt taekwondo with him and performed some taekwondo moves on the surface of the Moon.
  • Leader in Mars Mission Development: After retiring from NASA, Aldrin continues to be a leading advocate for space exploration, particularly missions to Mars. He has developed plans known as the "Mars Cycler", which is a transportation concept to carry astronauts to Mars and back to Earth with maximum efficiency.
  • MIT Alumnus: Before becoming an astronaut, Aldrin earned a Master of Science degree in Aeronautics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). This education greatly influenced his career at NASA and his contributions to space research and development.
  • Photography Hobby: During the Apollo 11 mission, Aldrin took several iconic photos that captured the historic moment of humanity's first landing on the Moon. Photography is one of Aldrin's hobbies, and he is known for capturing important moments in his space adventures.

Pete Conrad

Charles "Pete" Conrad Jr. (June 2, 1930 – July 8, 1999) was a NASA astronaut, aerospace engineer, naval officer, aviator, and test pilot from the United States. On the Apollo 12 mission, he became the third person to walk on the Moon. Conrad was selected in NASA's second group of astronauts.

Time in Space :

49 Days 3 Hours 38 Minutes

Pete Conrad

  • Nickname "Pete": Charles "Pete" Conrad Jr. earned his nickname during his time at the U.S. Naval Academy. It was there that his name "Charles" was quickly replaced by "Pete."
  • Shortest Astronaut to Walk on the Moon: Pete Conrad, at 5 feet 6 inches (167 cm), is the shortest astronaut to have walked on the Moon. Despite his relatively small stature, he made significant contributions to space exploration.
  • Commander of Apollo 12: Conrad commanded the Apollo 12 mission to the Moon, which was the second manned mission in the Apollo program to land on the lunar surface. He famously exclaimed "Whoopee! Man, that may have been a small one for Neil, but that's a long one for me," upon setting foot on the Moon.
  • Spacewalk Record: During the Gemini 5 mission, Conrad set a space endurance record by spending 8 days in space, and he also became the first person to perform a spacewalk during a Gemini mission.
  • Avid Golfer: Pete Conrad was an avid golfer and brought a six-iron golf club head attached to a lunar excavation tool handle during the Apollo 12 mission. He famously hit two golf balls on the lunar surface, making him the first (and so far, only) person to play golf on the Moon.
